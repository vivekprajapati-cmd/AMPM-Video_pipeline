# AM:PM — MASTER SCRIPT STRUCTURE TEMPLATE
**For use with AI script generation prompts**
Version 1.0 | Format: Vertical 9:16 | Duration: 60s | Platform: Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts

---

## HOW TO USE THIS TEMPLATE

This document is the **structural skeleton** to paste into any script generation prompt.
Fill in the `[BRACKETED FIELDS]` with the story-specific details, then feed the completed structure to the AI model.

Every section is required unless marked `[OPTIONAL]`.
Sections marked `[SYSTEM PROMPT ANCHOR]` are fixed — do not modify them between stories.

**Field definitions:**

| Field | What to write |
|---|---|
| VIDEO ID | Short code for asset naming. E.g. VIZAG-DRAMA-0526 |
| STORY | The factual headline this script is about — not the hook |
| FORMAT | How many avatars / characters are on screen |
| TEMPLATE | Story architecture (see Section 2) |
| TONE | Emotional register. Not sentiment — delivery mode |
| ANGLE | The editorial reframe. One sentence. This drives everything below. |

# SECTION 2 — TEMPLATE SELECTION

Choose **one** template. This determines the narrative architecture of the entire script.

---

## TEMPLATE A — EXPLAINER
**Use when:** Story is complex, audience needs context before opinion.
**Structure:** Context → Stakes → Pattern → Implication → CTA
**Best for:** Policy changes, institutional failures, economic shifts, regulatory decisions.
**Avatar setup:** Single avatar. Conversational, direct address.

```
0:00–0:04  HOOK (title card or spoken)
0:04–0:14  CONTEXT — what happened, in plain English
0:14–0:30  STAKES — why it matters, who is affected
0:30–0:46  PATTERN — what this reveals about a larger system
0:46–0:56  IMPLICATION — what comes next
0:56–1:00  AM:PM CARD
```

---

## TEMPLATE B — CONTRAST (MICRO-DRAMA)
**Use when:** Story has two legitimate perspectives that collide into a single truth.
**Structure:** Human cost ↔ Systemic failure → Convergence
**Best for:** Industrial accidents, policy gaps, governance failures, institutional negligence.
**Avatar setup:** Two characters. Split screen at convergence point.

```
0:00–0:02  TITLE CARD
0:02–0:08  CHARACTER 1 — personal, human, emotional (contained)
0:08–0:14  TEXT CARD — factual reset (the numbers)
0:14–0:28  CHARACTER 1 — builds to the systemic critique
0:28–0:42  CHARACTER 2 — institutional/systemic perspective
0:42–0:52  SPLIT SCREEN — both characters converge on the same truth
0:52–0:58  TEXT CARD — forward implication
0:58–1:00  AM:PM CARD
```

---

## TEMPLATE C — REVEAL
**Use when:** The real story is hidden inside a boring or misleading headline.
**Structure:** Surface → Dig → Truth → Why it was buried → CTA
**Best for:** Corporate announcements, government data, policy fine print, buried statistics.
**Avatar setup:** Single avatar. Conspiratorial but grounded tone.

```
0:00–0:04  HOOK — the misleading version of the story
0:04–0:16  SURFACE — what the headline says
0:16–0:36  DIG — what's actually in the document/data/record
0:36–0:50  TRUTH — what this actually means
0:50–0:58  WHY IT MATTERS — real consequence, not abstract
0:58–1:00  AM:PM CARD
```

---

## TEMPLATE D — TIMELINE
**Use when:** Story spans months or years and context is essential to understand the present moment.
**Structure:** Origin → Escalation → Inflection point → Now → Stakes
**Best for:** Legal cases, political careers, institutional decline, long-running crises.
**Avatar setup:** Single avatar with text card cutaways at each timeline beat.

```
0:00–0:04  HOOK
0:04–0:12  ORIGIN — where this started
0:12–0:22  ESCALATION — what should have happened, didn't
0:22–0:38  INFLECTION POINT — the moment that changed everything
0:38–0:50  NOW — current status, current stakes
0:50–0:58  IMPLICATION — what this sets up
0:58–1:00  AM:PM CARD
```

---

## TEMPLATE E — DEBATE
**Use when:** There is a genuine unresolved question with credible arguments on multiple sides.
**Structure:** The question → Side A → Side B → What the data actually shows → CTA
**Best for:** Policy debates, competing interpretations, contested narratives, court decisions.
**Avatar setup:** Two avatars OR single avatar presenting both sides clearly.

```
0:00–0:04  HOOK — the unresolved question
0:04–0:14  SIDE A — strongest version of position one
0:14–0:28  SIDE B — strongest version of position two
0:28–0:44  DATA LAYER — what the evidence shows (not an opinion)
0:44–0:56  WHAT'S ACTUALLY AT STAKE — the real consequence
0:56–1:00  AM:PM CARD
```

# SECTION 4 — FULL SCRIPT

Write the complete word-for-word script in chronological order.
All lines must be written as **spoken dialogue or caption text** — nothing in between.
Every beat must map directly to the storyboard in Section 7.

---

## SCRIPT FORMAT RULES

- **Title cards / text cards:** Written as captions, line by line. Mark duration.
- **Spoken lines:** Written exactly as the avatar will say them, with pause markers.
- **Pause markers:** `[beat — Xs]` where X is the pause in seconds.
- **Stage directions:** In parentheses. E.g. `(swipes phone)`, `(leans in)`, `(flips clipboard)`.
- **Split screen lines:** Both characters' lines written in parallel columns.
- **No filler phrases:** No "so basically", "right?", "you know", "um", "like".
- **No question-padding:** Don't end lines with rhetorical setup questions that go nowhere.

---

## SCRIPT BLOCK

```
TITLE CARD (0:00–0:0X)
"[Opening card text — punchy, 10 words max]"

[CHARACTER / SECTION LABEL] (0:0X–0:0X)
"[Spoken line 1.]
[Spoken line 2.]
[beat — Xs]
[Spoken line 3.]"

TEXT CARD (0:0X–0:0X)
"[Factual reset — 2–3 lines. Data-driven. Plain English.]
[Line 2]
[Line 3]"

[CHARACTER / SECTION LABEL] (0:0X–0:0X)
"[Extended spoken passage with pause markers as needed.]"

SPLIT SCREEN — BOTH CHARACTERS (0:0X–0:0X)
[CHARACTER 1]: "[Line]"
[CHARACTER 2]: "[Line]"
BOTH: "[Convergence line — spoken together or back-to-back within 0.5s]"

TEXT CARD (0:0X–0:0X)
"[Forward implication — 2–3 lines. What's at stake now.]"

FINAL CARD (0:58–1:00)
AM:PM logo + "News, decoded. Twice a day."
[Swipe up / CTA line]
```

# SECTION 5 — LIPSYNC SCRIPTS

Each take = one continuous audio recording session.
One take per scene segment per character.
Written exactly as the audio file will be recorded — no annotations, no stage directions (except silent pause markers).

---

## LIPSYNC FORMAT RULES

- Each take is a **separate recording** — label clearly.
- **Pause markers in lipsync:** Written as `[pause Xs]` or `[beat Xs]`. These are silences in the audio file.
- **Action cues for video sync:** Written separately as `[action: X]` — these go to the Higgsfield prompt, not the voice recording.
- No character name in the recording script. The file is the character.
- Line breaks = natural breath pauses.
- Do not include text card lines in lipsync scripts.

- Then **lighting** (type, direction, quality).

# SECTION 8 — HIGGSFIELD LIPSYNC PROMPTS

One lipsync prompt per character. Used in Higgsfield for avatar video generation.
These are motion + identity prompts — more detailed than image prompts.

---

## LIPSYNC PROMPT FORMAT RULES

- Open with **character action description** — what they are doing in the shot.
- **Lighting** — same as image prompt. Consistency is critical.
- **Delivery** — how they speak. Precise.
- **Micromotion** — head movement, blinking, gestures. Be specific and minimal.
- **Shot lock** — always "Locked medium close-up, chest-up, eye-level. No zoom, no shake."
- **Background** — one-line description.
- **Exclusion block** — always identical. See master exclusion below.
- **Negative prompt** — always identical. See master negative prompt below.

---

## HIGGSFIELD LIPSYNC PROMPT — CHARACTER 1

```
Generate a lipsync video of [CHARACTER DESCRIPTION — appearance + setting].
[Lighting description].
[Speaking style — delivery, tone, emotional register].
[Micro-motion: head movement, blinking, gestures — be specific, keep minimal].
[Shot: Locked medium close-up, chest-up, eye-level. No zoom, no shake.]
Realistic skin texture. [Background one-line description].
Exclude: no captions, no subtitles, no text overlay, no logos, no watermark, no UI, no graphics, no words in frame.
```

# APPENDIX — WRITING RULES FOR SCRIPT GENERATION

These rules apply to any AI model or human writer generating the script content.
Paste this block into the system prompt of your AI script generation call.

---

## EDITORIAL VOICE
Write like: investigative journalist, behavioral economist, political analyst.
Never write like: news anchor, activist, fan page, political spokesperson.

Tone: Sharp. Composed. Specific. Direct.
Avoid: Sensationalism, patriotic framing, collective resolve language, moral panic.

---

## LINE-BY-LINE RULES

- Every line must do one job. If it does two, break it into two.
- No sentence longer than 15 words spoken aloud.
- No filler transitions: no "so", "right?", "you know", "basically", "essentially".
- No passive voice unless the passivity is the point.
- Numbers beat adjectives. "Eight workers" beats "several workers". "Third year running" beats "repeatedly".
- Questions only when the question is the point. Not as setup padding.
- Pauses earn their place. A beat is not decoration — it's a beat where something lands.
- The spoken line must survive being read aloud fast. If it trips, rewrite it.

---

## STRUCTURAL RULES

- The HOOK must create a contradiction, a gap, or a cost the audience didn't know existed.
- The TEXT CARD is a factual reset — data only, no opinion, no framing.
- The CHARACTER ARC must move. The character must arrive somewhere different from where they started.
- The CONVERGENCE LINE (Template B) must feel inevitable — both characters had to get there.
- The FINAL TEXT CARD must be forward-looking. Not summary — implication.
- The AM:PM CARD is never the climax. The climax is always the line before it.

---

## WHAT THE SCRIPT IS NOT

- Not a summary of the news article.
- Not a timeline of events.
- Not a hot take or opinion piece.
- Not a government press release reworded.
- Not safe, hedged, or both-sides.

---

## WHAT THE SCRIPT IS

- The real story inside the headline.
- The human cost made specific.
- The systemic failure named precisely.
- The implication that the audience can do something with.
